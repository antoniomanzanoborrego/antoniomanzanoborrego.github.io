Procedural Generation - Books of Horror

keyboard_arrow_down

Reinventing Procedural Horror

Books of Horror is a procedural psychological horror game currently in development with Save Game Studio, slated for release in 2026. Rooted in Lovecraft's quote, "the oldest and strongest emotion of mankind is fear, and the oldest and strongest kind of fear is fear of the unknown", the game forces players to improvise in unpredictable environments.

The Challenge: Beyond Pre-built Rooms

Most procedural systems simply snap together pre-made rooms. For Books of Horror, I aimed for a more organic and challenging experience where the architecture itself acts as an antagonist.

The Solution: Algorithmic Architecture

I developed a system that constructs dungeons from the ground up, cell by cell. The algorithm manages room shapes and categorizes instantiation points based on their functionality (walls vs. room centers), validating essential escape routes before finalizing the layout.

Benchmark: 100+ rooms / 16-30ms logic time

Technical Optimization

Performance was non-negotiable. The system generates 15 rooms in just 30 ms. Logically, dungeons exceeding 100 rooms are processed in under 30 ms.

Level Design Systems - The Last Witch

keyboard_arrow_down

Structure and Pacing in 3D Environments

The Last Witch is a 3D action-platformer focused on dynamic combat and contained exploration. The main design challenge was structuring open environments to control player tension and prevent disorientation without relying on intrusive UI elements.

Macro-Design: Global Map Overview

The general map is structured into interconnected sectors via natural bottlenecks. This regulates gameplay pacing, alternating free exploration freedom with controlled points of no return enforced by terrain geometry.

Micro-Design: Learning Curve and Onboarding

To avoid overwhelming the player, mechanics are introduced in an isolated and controlled manner across two distinct initial zones:

Zone 1: Freedom, Combat, and Environmental Stealth

The player spawns in the center with total freedom of movement. Points of interest branch toward enemies and a high-elevation block hiding the progression key.

Design rewards observation: one enemy patrols and stops facing backwards, organically inviting the player to execute a surprise attack. A second enemy patrols with a stealth zone at the end of their path. Paths leading to optional items are discovered naturally while exploring near the enemies.

Zone 2: Platforming Introduction and Hazards

Once initial tension clears, the second part transitions into an isolated environment focused purely on precision platforming to gauge distances.

Spikes are introduced as a clear lethal environmental hazard, alongside a classic design duality: a visible optional item that tempts the player into a higher-difficulty alternative path.

Design Intentionality

Through precise study of heights, volumes, and AI behaviors during the blockout phase, players learn the game's rules intuitively without relying on intrusive text tutorials.

Custom Narrative Tools & Pipeline - Tollebant Cor

keyboard_arrow_down

Empowering Narrative Design

During my time leading the technical implementation of Tollebant Cor, I was tasked with translating complex game mechanics into functional code. Recognizing that narrative writing and game balance were evolving faster than the codebase, I developed a custom external tool pipeline to decouple game logic from content creation.

Unblocking Content Creators

To avoid bottlenecks and eliminate the team's dependency on programmers for narrative tweaks, the goal was to allow designers and writers to author branching dialogues, manage game states, and trigger gameplay events without touching a single line of code.

Custom data structure mapping dialogue nodes, choices, and event calls.

I designed a light, intuitive spreadsheet-to-JSON workflow. Content creators manage dialogues, branching paths, and narrative decisions through custom tables, which are parsed dynamically by Unity at runtime.

The system supports advanced functions directly from content sheets:

  • Dynamic Function Triggering: Changing scenes, character states, and unlocking SMS conversations or special CG art.
  • Relationship & Variable Tracking: Modifying affinity metrics (e.g., Sumar/Restar character stats) based on player choices.
  • Localization & Multi-branching: Managing complex dialogue trees and variable-dependent choices effortlessly.

Project Impact

This tool pipeline empowered narrative designers and translators to iterate and test gameplay fully autonomously, completely isolating content creation from code delivery and ensuring development progressed smoothly even during programming freezes.
